Figure Stone

   After Sally Mann's "The Ditch"

During the bad months I kept a fishbowl
  of black rocks on the coffeetable

Home to an ailing moss
  sheared by aquarium's filter

Moving to dust, I cracked open
  along a faultline in the glass

No one noticed sand darkening
  as there was already so much water

The grays--faceted as fly's eye--
  washed out green & dried violet

Dyeing the towel of an onlooker
  and her mouth's concerned twist

Dead the moss turned to wet brick
  The boy a crab in the waves

So silent I turned on all the lights
  before seeing him sapped first

Of color, then of sound
