Independent Study


We’re studying stones when I find what you’re seeking.
I hold the shovel between your spurts of digging. You say
to survive the tissue must have been buried all at once.
Don’t bother with the paperwork. Any bone might ruffle
the sand, but we have precious little of the soft stuff.

You fill the bucket I hold with red clay, once and maybe
still alive. I sift for a trace of insects, proof of movement
in the Great Already, but you’re carving stairs in the pit.
Now a desk. Soon a room where you’ll bring me.

This is the problem with digging for digging’s sake,
with a kiss on the hand of a much younger woman.
Unless rock solidifies around bacteria they will live
for dinner parties, any chance run-ins with the flesh.

I am lucky to be burdened with all this equipment,
lucky it fits in one hard-shell case. The last you see
of me is the skeleton of a whale strapped to a minivan.
I drive away with everything useful. I leave your
blacklight behind for the next.
