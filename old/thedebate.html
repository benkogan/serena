The Debate


To me the candidates look like horses led
into a ring but behind me someone points out
the man’s resemblance to a wolf, the way

his neck retracts into his ruff and his teeth
get naked, graze her flank, leave welts like
red ornaments. I trace a fence with the crowd.

Smoke billows from a distant lot— exhaust,
probably, but I draw closer to the nice jackets
ahead, scanning the jumbotron for her magnified

visage as balloons flood the stage. On the screen
she stands encircled by buttons, themselves
embellished with rhinestones. They applaud.

Decked with rubies, a flag congratulates her
open-armed on the evening. When it’s done
everyone gets to go home with a tote bag

except for her. In my notes I put down
four words I’m only starting to understand
the meaning of: horses don’t get gambling.
