Shorts with Inner Brief

I.
Shortly after moving west I grew breasts unlike
 the modest assumptions I sported in college.
Questions of form became questions of function.
 Are these straps load-bearing. Will a dog
evade the leash. How many inches between cup
 & bust’s width. Does a tree make its own shade
or does the storm fill seedbeds with darkness
 when it passes. Can I run in it. I started running
when I arrived, then the bra incident. Or did the tits
 come first. Or the linty bits of pollen clogging
the pond. Or the ruins admiring their ample
 reflection. Did I bring the damn things with me
or were they already here, splayed by the ramp
 as if thrown from a car. How did I attach
this extra flesh to my chest. Where it sits was once
 an air conditioned room. Now isn’t heavy exactly
but the heat. To evade it I consult the oak
 who shivers above and beneath the ground.

II.
Shade is the leg of a pig.
 Like us, ants will
come a long way to eat
 and to bring back.
